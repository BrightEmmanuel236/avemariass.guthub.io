About Us

Our School's Background information

Sr. Therese Rwija started a school leavers project at Rwibaale, Kyenjojo district in mid 1990s. this, she did with the help of Bishop Magambo. the school leavers project would admit school dropouts from Kyenjojo and Kyaka. It was a vocational school and therefore attendants were taught skills like carpentry, hair dressing, building and others. However, the project wasn’t so successful. I visited the place and was not happy with the project. the number of learners was miserably small and I somehow felt that we were not helping the locals as expected.

My greatest concern was the girl child. the nearest schools at that time were Kisojo ss and Wekomiire ss. I somehow felt that the boys could walk to these schools but i feared for the girls. By that time, I was a member of the governing council for the Daughter of the child Jesus- commonly known as Banyatereza sisters. I presented my concerns about Rwibaale and the council suggested that we engage the local leaders of Rwibaale about the matter.

Consequently, we had a meeting with the entire narrative community. They suggested to us that we start a secondary school and a health facility if we were to help them. There was a man called Mr. Kahigwa, the then chairperson LC III. He was very supportive throughout the process.

in 1999, the secondary school begun. the pioneer students were 12. they were well behaved students, eager to learn and excelled at the end of four year course. in their maiden sitting, they did their UCE examinations at Kyenjojo ss. The following year, the school got school got its center number.
i suggest that the school be called Ave Maria for a number of reasons. Firstly, i wished to maintain Sr.Theresa Rwija’s interests. she loved the character.

in 1990’s and early 2000s we had a problem of ADF rebels in the region. The first school leaver vocational school had somehow failed. we did not have serious donors at the time and therefore we lacked money to run many of the operations. With all these challenges clearly in sight, i proposed Ave Maria to call on the unending mercy of our school, in order to overcome these challenges. i was right. Mother Mary has not disappointed us. the motto, ‘Aim Higher’ was premised on the bare fact that the people of Rwibaale needed t aim high so that the school could prosper. it was a call to all of us in that establishment to render unwavering labor if we were to succeed.
